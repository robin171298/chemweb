Help & User Guide

Welcome

Welcome to the nervous system drug database. This page explains how to use the website and how to understand the information shown.

🔍 Browsing the Database

On the overview page, you can see the five main drug classes:

  • Benzodiazepines
  • Antidepressants
  • Antipsychotics
  • Stimulants
  • Antiepileptics

Click on any class to view all compounds that belong to it. You can also view a general list of compounds without filtering.

🧪 Understanding the Drug Information

Each compound entry contains:

  • Name and PubChem CID – for clear identification
  • Molecular formula and molecular weight – basic chemical properties
  • XLogP and polar surface area – indicators of lipophilicity and brain penetration
  • Hydrogen bond donors and acceptors – important for drug-likeness
  • Mechanism of action – how the drug works biologically
  • Main medical use – what the drug is used for in medicine

These properties are commonly used in medicinal chemistry and pharmacology to describe and compare drugs.

💊 Lipinski’s Rule of Five

The database includes properties used in Lipinski’s Rule of Five, a guideline for estimating whether a compound is suitable for oral administration. Compounds that meet most or all of these rules are more likely to have good absorption in the human body.

🧠 Focus on Nervous System Drugs

This database focuses on nervous system drugs because they play a central role in treating:

  • Mental health disorders
  • Neurological diseases
  • Epilepsy
  • Sleep and anxiety disorders
  • Cognitive and attention-related conditions

These drugs are among the most widely used and scientifically studied medications worldwide.

🧬 Molecular Structure View

For many compounds, a 2D molecular structure image is shown. These images are provided via PubChem and help visualize how the molecule is built.

⚠️ Important Disclaimer

This database is intended for educational and scientific purposes only. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for medical decisions.
